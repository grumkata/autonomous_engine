# SKILL: writing/longform — Long-Form Prose

## Purpose
Guides implementation and documentation agents producing essays, articles,
book chapters, reports, scripts, or any extended narrative prose.

## Quality bar
- Minimum 400 words unless the task explicitly specifies shorter.
- Every section must have a clear topic sentence that states its purpose.
- Claims must be grounded — hedging without evidence ("some argue") is a red flag.
- Active voice preferred. Passive is acceptable for emphasis, not as a default.
- Transitions between paragraphs must be explicit (not just whitespace).
- No filler phrases: "it is important to note", "in conclusion", "needless to say".

## Structure requirements
Produce your output with clear headings unless the deliverable is specifically
flowing prose (fiction, essays without sections). For structured documents:
- Title (descriptive, not generic)
- Introduction that states the thesis or purpose in the first paragraph
- Body with labeled sections
- Conclusion that synthesises, not just summarises

## Voice and register
Match register to the project type:
- Technical documentation → precise, neutral, second-person where instructional
- Research writing → hedged but specific; cite assumption vs evidence clearly
- Creative/narrative → voice consistent with established project style
- Marketing → direct, benefit-led, second-person

## What to include in findings
- The word count and section breakdown of what you produced.
- Key editorial decisions (tone, register, what you included vs excluded).
- Any factual claims you made that would need verification by a research agent.

## Common failure modes — avoid these
- Starting every paragraph with "The" or "This".
- Producing a bullet-point list when prose was requested.
- Writing an introduction that repeats the task description verbatim.
- Ending abruptly without synthesis.
- Padding with definitions of terms the audience already knows.
